01 / WHAT

What is Nothing?

Nothing is a cryptographic bearer instrument — think of it like a signed banknote that only the right person can read, traveling through a network where no one can tell what it is or where it came from.

Traditional digital money requires a ledger: a list of who owns what, maintained by someone you trust (a bank, a blockchain). Nothing requires no such list. It carries its own proof of validity inside itself, sealed against anyone who isn't the intended recipient.

Before it arrives it is literally nothing — an opaque blob of bytes with no ticker symbol, no account number, no asset class. The moment the recipient verifies it, it becomes something. Not a second earlier.

⌀

No Ledger

There is no record of who owns what. No database anyone can query. No account required. The token is the only record, held only by its owner.

◈

No Identity in Transit

On the wire, the token is double-encrypted noise. Even if intercepted, it reveals nothing about what it is, who sent it, or who receives it.

◉

Settlement = Classification

Nothing becomes a coin only at the moment a zero-knowledge proof of valid ownership is verified. That event is the birth of the coin. Not before.

02 / HOW

How it works

01

Mint

A random 32-byte serial number is generated. The serial is blinded — transformed with a secret blinding factor — and sent to the mint for signing. The mint signs it using RSA without ever seeing the actual serial. Then the signature is unblinded, producing a valid RSA signature over the serial that the mint can never link back to the signing request.

This is Chaum's blind signature (1983). The same mechanism proposed for anonymous digital cash decades before Bitcoin existed.

sig = unblind(mint_sign(blind(serial, r)), r)
verify(serial, sig, mint_pubkey) → true
02

Transfer

The signed token is sealed to the recipient's X25519 public key using libsodium's sealed-box construction — an ephemeral Diffie-Hellman key exchange followed by XSalsa20-Poly1305 encryption. The sender is anonymous; the ephemeral key is discarded immediately.

The sealed token travels over a libp2p mesh — a peer-to-peer network with Noise protocol encryption at the transport layer. Every byte on the wire is already ciphertext. The inner payload is ciphertext inside ciphertext.

sealed = libsodium_sealed_box(token, recipient_x25519_pk)
transport = noise_encrypt(sealed) over tcp
03

Settle

The holder generates a Groth16 zero-knowledge proof — a 192-byte cryptographic object that proves they know a valid (serial, signature) pair without revealing either.

The circuit verifies the RSA blind signature in-circuit using 2048-bit bigint arithmetic inside the BN254 elliptic curve field (~210,000 constraints). A Poseidon hash of the serial acts as a nullifier — publicly recorded to prevent double-spending without identifying the token.

When the Groth16 pairing check passes, Nothing has classified as a coin. Not a second before.

proof = groth16_prove(serial, sig, modulus)
verify(proof, modulus, nullifier) → ✓ coin
